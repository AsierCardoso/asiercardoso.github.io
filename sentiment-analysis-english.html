Project Overview

This project involved comprehensive sentiment analysis of Airbnb and TripAdvisor reviews to predict user satisfaction on a 1-9 scale. The analysis combined traditional machine learning algorithms with modern generative AI approaches to achieve superior performance in sentiment classification.

Methodology

The project employed a multi-stage approach: data collection and preprocessing, TF-IDF vectorization, multiple classification algorithms (Naive Bayes, Random Forest, Decision Tree, Logistic Regression), and generative AI models (Ollama's Gemma2:2b) for comparative analysis.

Data Analysis & Visualization

Rating Comparison: New York vs Hong Kong

New York maintains a slight but consistent advantage in average rating (9.42 vs 9.2), demonstrating successful service differentiation and customer satisfaction strategies that create sustainable competitive advantage in the market.

Accommodation Type Distribution

Both cities show 80% apartments, but Hong Kong presents greater typological diversity with categories like Service Apartments and Hostels, indicating a more segmented market approach that captures diverse customer segments and maximizes revenue opportunities.

Average Price by Accommodation Type

Hong Kong offers generally lower prices ($87 vs $134 for apartments), with economic options like Hostels ($50) targeting budget-conscious travelers, demonstrating effective market positioning that captures both premium and value-conscious customer segments.

Price vs Rating Relationship

No strong correlation between price and rating. Even accommodations over $800 show only slightly higher ratings (0.2 points). This suggests that customers are generally satisfied with the quality they receive relative to the price they pay, indicating effective value perception across all price segments.

Rating Evolution by Year

New York has historically maintained better ratings than Hong Kong (~0.3 point difference). In 2019, the gap widened notably with NY approaching 10 and HK dropping to 9, indicating successful implementation of customer experience improvements and operational excellence initiatives.

Monthly Rating Evolution

Both cities show monthly stability, but New York improves in December and January, while Hong Kong experiences a significant drop in May, revealing seasonal opportunity patterns that can inform targeted marketing campaigns and resource allocation strategies.

Monthly Price Analysis

New York maintains relatively stable prices year-round, indicating consistent demand and pricing power. Hong Kong experiences a price peak in August, demonstrating successful dynamic pricing strategies that maximize revenue during peak demand periods.

Superhost Impact on Ratings

Superhosts consistently achieve higher ratings: Hong Kong (9.67 vs 9.05) and New York (9.65 vs 9.26), validating the Superhost program as an effective quality assurance system that drives customer satisfaction and creates premium value propositions.

Technical Implementation

Data Processing
  • Stratified sampling by city (64% NY / 36% HK)
  • TF-IDF vectorization with unigrams
  • Rating normalization to 1-9 scale
  • 70/15/15 train/dev/test split
Machine Learning Models
  • Random Forest (F1: 0.87)
  • Decision Tree (F1: 0.83)
  • Logistic Regression (F1: 0.73)
  • Naive Bayes (F1: 0.65)
Generative AI
  • Ollama Gemma2:2b model
  • Zero-shot, One-shot, Few-shot strategies
  • Parallel processing with ProcessPoolExecutor
  • Automatic translation to English
Performance Metrics
  • Random Forest: 91% Precision
  • One-shot strategy: Best MSE
  • Few-shot: 10% improvement in negative detection
  • Comprehensive evaluation across all classes

Key Achievements

Model Performance
  • Random Forest achieved 91% precision and 87% F1-score
  • Significant improvement over baseline Naive Bayes (65% F1)
  • Effective handling of class imbalance through stratified sampling
Business Insights
  • Identified seasonal patterns in pricing and ratings
  • Confirmed Superhost status as quality indicator
  • Discovered effective value perception across all price segments
  • Revealed customer satisfaction is quality-price balanced
